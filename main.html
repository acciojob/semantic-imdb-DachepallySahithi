Summary

The Shawshank Redemption is a 1994 American drama film written and directed by Frank Darabont, based on the 1982 Stephen King novella Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption. It tells the story of Andy Dufresne, a banker who is sentenced to life in Shawshank State Penitentiary for the murders of his wife and her lover, despite his claims of innocence. Over the years, he forms a friendship with fellow inmate Ellis "Red" Redding, and becomes involved in a money-laundering operation run by the prison warden.

Actors

  • Tim Robbins as Andy Dufresne
  • Morgan Freeman as Ellis "Red" Redding
  • Bob Gunton as Warden Samuel Norton
  • Clancy Brown as Captain Byron Hadley
  • James Whitmore as Brooks Hatlen

These actors delivered powerful performances that contributed to the film's enduring popularity and critical acclaim.
